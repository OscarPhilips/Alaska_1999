June 8, 6185 miles

With Merry Lou on her way back home, we decided to play a little today with a session of fishing for King Salmon. After fishing for a few hours we did do just a little traveling today also.
We went out with a guide and a couple from Colorado, and caught 3 King Salmon (the daily bag limit is one per person). Mine is the small one (23 lbs).
We did have another beautiful day, few clouds, no rain, wonderful mid 60's temperature.

We hope we get to leave this guy behind, but I doubt it. The mosquito is often times referred to as Alaska's state bird, but I think Canada has also adopted it as their national icon.

We found a cabin about 100 miles east of Palmer for the night, traveling some 207 miles.

Wildlife for the day included: we were skinked - nothing of special note.

Tomorrow will be a travel day, going toward Dawson City.
